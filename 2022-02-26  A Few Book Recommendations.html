2022-22-26 A Few Book Recommendations


Looking for something interesting to read? Try these:
  • The Woman Who Smashed Codes - Jason Fagone
    A well-written look at a fascinating couple, with insights on cryptography, code-breaking, US intelligence from 1917 to the sixties. Highly recommended.
  • The Age of AI: And Our Human Future - Henry Kissinger, Eric Schmidt, Daniel Huttenlocher
    An interesting look at the impacts of AI on our society and our national security.
  • Linux Basics for Hackers - OccupyTheWeb
    A great introduction to cyber security and the Linux apps and commands available to us, with details on loading a special Linux instance as a sandbox.
  • God Human Animal Machine - Meghan O'Gieblyn
    This book examines various philosophical and theological questions. What differentiates humans from animals, and from machines? Are we emulating God when we create thinking machines in our image? Do computers “think”?
